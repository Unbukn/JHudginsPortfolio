About Me

Why I Started Coding:

I was 14 years old watching youtube videos on how to host your own private server for a video game called, “Runescape.” I had no idea if I would ever figure everything out but I had an idea of what I wanted to accomplish. Eventually after days of trial and error, I figured out how to get the game running locally. Now let’s be clear, this experience was primarily me making minor changes to open-source code, and I knew little about the mountain of files I had downloaded to my PC.

How Everything Changed:

Fortunately, through this process I discovered a great deal about how the game was architected. After combing over the executable files I understood the relationship between the server and client(s). Next, I learned that changes wouldn’t appear in the game unless I ran the compiler. Later, I managed to figure out how to change my firewall settings to allow the server to initialize to an internet-facing port (I know, super dangerous stuff). I had my own community of faithful users vested in changes I made from the base code. This time in life is really what initially piqued my interest in understanding the inner-workings of computer applications. 

Where I'm at Now:

Fast forward four years and I’m working to automate software tests at General Dynamics Electric Boat (GDEB). This was my first taste at a practical application of my skills as an engineer. I learned how to keep a system online, create backups, configuration manage products, reduce redundancies, generate SMS alerts, coordinate health checks, and create reports based on analytical data. I think that I will make a good software engineer because I have spent countless hours professionally and independently researching, testing, tinkering, and fiddling with various IDEs, developer tools, and API, and I enjoyed every second. My understanding of software engineering is a bit fragmented at the moment, but with proper instruction, I’m confident that I can enhance my confidence and employability as a software engineer.
